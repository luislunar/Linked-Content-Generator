This one pricing mistake quietly kills small businesses on GSA Schedule.

They negotiate their rates down to win the contract. Then they can't compete on task orders.

Here's what actually happens:

→ **GSA negotiates your Basis of Award price.** Whatever you charge your best commercial customer becomes your price ceiling on the Schedule.
→ **If you discount too aggressively to get approved**, your GSA price is now your lowest commercial price — permanently locked in until your next modification.
→ **Agencies compare Schedule prices across vendors.** If your rate is artificially low, you either win at a margin that doesn't work or you get passed over by agencies who assume low rate = low quality.
→ **Price modifications take 3–6 months.** You can't react quickly to market changes. Getting the initial price right matters more than getting on the Schedule fast.
→ **The right move:** negotiate to protect margin, not to get the lowest rate. GSA isn't your biggest customer on day one — you need room to grow into it.

I've seen companies get their Schedule approved at 40% below market rate because they thought it would help them win more. It didn't. It just locked them into a price that didn't work.
