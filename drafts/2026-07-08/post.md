The wrong question isn't "when does the RFP drop?"

The right one is: "Am I already in the CO's inbox?"

Most federal acquisitions are shaped before the public solicitation ever posts. Sources Sought notices, industry days, one-on-one informational meetings — this is where contracting officers build their mental shortlist. Small businesses wait for SAM.gov to alert them. Strong contractors have been cultivating the relationship for six months.

By the time the formal RFP drops, the CO already knows who can deliver. They still have to run the competition. But a cold proposal from an unknown vendor is fighting uphill from page one.

The move isn't to respond faster to solicitations. It's to show up before the requirement is even funded — respond to Sources Sought notices, attend agency industry days, request informational meetings early.

Your GSA Schedule gets you in the door. Showing up early is what wins.
