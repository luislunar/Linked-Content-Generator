This draft tests a `myth-bust` hook in carousel format on the `myths` topic. The opening line drops a specific, recognizable false belief — "You need federal contracts before GSA will approve you" — which is the exact thought that keeps qualified ICP companies on the sideline longest. Framing it as a direct quote invites instant identification: readers either nod in recognition (they believed it) or forward it to someone who does.

The carousel format was chosen because three myths with point-by-point rebuttals is genuinely multi-part content — each slide delivers a discrete, complete idea rather than padding a single point. The swipe mechanic also extends dwell time, which LinkedIn's algorithm rewards. The close slide's community question ("Which myth almost stopped you?") targets comments specifically: it asks for a personal confession rather than an opinion, which typically yields higher-quality, longer replies. Expected gains: comments (personal stories tend to surface) and reactions (myth-busting + validation is a high-share combo).
