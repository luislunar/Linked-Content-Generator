She had everything. Active GSA Schedule. Clean SAM registration. A sharp capability statement.

And zero awards after two years.

She came to me frustrated — not angry, just exhausted. She'd done what everyone told her to do. Got on the Schedule. Kept her SAM current. Showed up to industry days. But RFQs weren't landing, and the few she responded to went nowhere. She assumed it was a marketing problem or a past performance problem. It wasn't.

The issue was buried in her pricing. When she originally filed her GSA application, her Basis of Award pricing wasn't set correctly relative to her actual commercial rates. The result: her Schedule rates were sitting 20–30% above what comparable primes in her NAICS had negotiated down to over years of modifications. Contracting officers' automated tools were filtering her out before a human ever read her proposal. She was invisible — and she didn't know it.

We filed a Schedule modification to correct the rates. About 60 days to process.

She had her first award 90 days after the modification landed.

Two years of patience. One pricing audit. Three months of work. That's the whole story.

Getting on the Schedule is step one. Getting the **right price** on the Schedule is step zero.

If you're on the GSA Schedule and RFQ responses have been quiet for 6+ months — when did you last compare your rates to what primes in your NAICS code are actually charging?
