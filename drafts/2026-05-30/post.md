# Post — 2026-05-30

Nobody talks enough about how much federal contract money flows through the GSA Schedule every single year.

$30 billion+. That's the rough annual spend through GSA Multiple Award Schedule contracts — and it goes to companies that took the time to get listed.

Most small business owners I talk to have no idea the number is that high. They think the federal market is reserved for the Lockheed Martins and the Raytheon-sized primes. They're wrong.

The GSA Schedule — also called the MAS (Multiple Award Schedule) — is specifically designed as a streamlined buying channel for federal agencies to work *with* small businesses. Agencies use it to skip the full competitive procurement process. They pull from pre-vetted vendors who are already on contract.

If you're not on that list, you're not even in the room when the buying happens.

The win rate isn't guaranteed — it never is in federal contracting. But the access it creates is real. We've watched clients go from zero federal footprint to their first federal award, and it started with getting on the Schedule.

$30 billion flows somewhere. The only question is whether your company is positioned to compete for any of it.
