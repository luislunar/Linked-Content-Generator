Nobody talks enough about where pipeline actually comes from in GovCon.

Most small businesses find out about an opportunity when it's already posted as a solicitation on SAM.gov. By that point, the agency usually knows who they want. You're bidding late into a pre-wired process.

Real pipeline starts 12–18 months earlier. Here's where to look:

1. **Sources Sought notices** — The government posts these to test the market before writing the solicitation. Responding gets your company name in the file. That's the whole goal.

2. **SAM.gov NAICS + keyword alerts** — Set up automated email alerts filtered to your specific NAICS codes and a tight keyword list. Broad daily sweeps miss the niche recompetes that are actually winnable.

3. **Recompete tracking** — Every current contract has a public end date. USAspending.gov shows who holds it and when it expires. A contract ending in 18 months is a warm lead available right now.

4. **Industry Days** — Agencies host these to gauge market capability before writing the RFQ. Attending gets you face time with the contracting officer. Skipping gets you nothing.

5. **Incumbent mapping** — Before you pursue any opportunity, look up who currently holds the contract. That company is either your main competitor or your best teaming target.

The agencies aren't hiding anything. They publish everything. Most small businesses just don't know where to look before the clock runs out.
