Before you write off that RFQ because you don't have federal past performance — there's a move you might be skipping.

One of my clients runs a 7-year-old IT consulting firm. Steady commercial revenue, solid margins, a team of 22. But zero federal contracts. Every GSA solicitation they found had the same line: "demonstrated federal past performance required." They thought they had to wait.

They didn't. They teamed.

We helped them structure a teaming agreement with a small prime that already held an active GSA MAS contract. Within six months, they were listed as a named subcontractor on two running task orders. When they came back to us a year later to finalize their own GSA Schedule application, they had three documented federal performance references to submit — all earned, all real.

Zero to three federal references in a year. Not by waiting for someone to hand them a contract, but by getting strategic about who they aligned with from the start.

Past performance is a chicken-and-egg problem. Teaming is how you crack it.
