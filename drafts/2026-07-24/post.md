I talked to a business owner last year who had spent three months getting everything ready for her GSA Schedule application.

Strong financials. Solid past performance. NAICS codes lined up. She was practically ready to submit — and proud of it.

Then we looked at her pricing.

She had built her GSA rates exactly the way she built commercial quotes — based on what felt competitive in her market. No Basis of Award documentation. No comparison to what similar vendors were already charging on the Schedule. Just a rate card that had worked for private clients.

Here's the part most people don't realize: those GSA rates aren't easy to change after approval. You're locking in your margins, your competitiveness on GSA Advantage, and your room to negotiate task orders — for years.

Under-price, and you've handed the government a discount you'll carry forever. Over-price relative to your Schedule competitors, and contracting officers move on without a second look.

We paused the submission. Ran a full pricing audit. Rebuilt the rate structure before anything went to GSA.

Nine months after approval, she landed her first federal contract. Margins intact.

The paperwork is what people talk about. The pricing is what quietly determines whether the contract actually works for you.
