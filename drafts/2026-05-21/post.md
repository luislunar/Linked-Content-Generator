Nobody warns you that past performance is a chicken-and-egg problem.

You need federal contracts to build past performance. You need past performance to win federal contracts. And if you've never worked with the government before, it can feel like the door is permanently sealed shut.

I worked with an IT services company a while back. Two years in business, $2.4M in commercial revenue, clean financials, a capable team. By every objective measure, they were ready for the federal market. But every RFP they opened wanted at least three prior federal contracts. They had zero.

We helped them structure a teaming agreement with a seasoned prime. The prime led the proposal. Our client delivered the technical work. Eight months later, they had their first past performance citation — their name on a completed federal contract. Within eighteen months, they bid as a prime on a similar scope and won.

Teaming isn't a consolation prize for companies that "aren't ready." It's the fastest legitimate path from zero federal history to your first real foothold.

The ceiling you're bumping into isn't the government's. It's the strategy you're using.

If you're hitting the past-performance wall, what's your current plan? Are you exploring teaming, or does it still feel like a second-tier option to you?
