"You can't get on the GSA Schedule without federal past performance." I've heard this one so many times — and it's stopped a lot of qualified businesses from even applying.

Here's what's actually true.

→ GSA Schedule requires **commercial** past performance — not federal contracts. Your existing B2B clients count.
→ The core bar is two years in business with demonstrable revenue. No government award required.
→ Companies brand-new to federal contracting get approved every month. The Schedule was designed to bring in new market entrants.
→ What actually kills applications: incomplete pricing documentation, wrong NAICS codes, and poorly structured past performance narratives.
→ The barrier to GSA approval is paperwork complexity — not your lack of government history.

The federal market isn't reserved for contractors who've already cracked the door.
