# Federal Contracting Cheat Sheet: 20 Acronyms That Decide Whether You Win

Every RFP, Schedule solicitation, and contracting officer email is written in acronym. Miss the meaning of one and you bid wrong. Here are the 20 that actually decide outcomes — with the 1-line definition and the trap most small businesses fall into.

## The 20

1. **GSA** — General Services Administration. Runs the Schedules program.
2. **MAS** — Multiple Award Schedule. The umbrella covering all current GSA Schedules (consolidated 2020).
3. **SIN** — Special Item Number. The sub-category inside MAS your product fits. Pick wrong = invisible on GSA Advantage.
4. **UEI** — Unique Entity Identifier. Replaced DUNS in 2022. Required on SAM.gov before any federal work.
5. **SAM** — System for Award Management (sam.gov). You must be registered and **active** to bid.
6. **NAICS** — North American Industry Classification System. Your business's industry code. Determines size standards.
7. **PSC** — Product Service Code. Federal buyers' classification — different from NAICS. Your SIN maps to one.
8. **CAGE** — Commercial and Government Entity code. Auto-assigned via SAM registration.
9. **RFP / RFQ / RFI** — Request for Proposal / Quotation / Information. RFI = no bid yet. RFQ = bid. RFP = bid + technical approach.
10. **SOW / PWS** — Statement of Work / Performance Work Statement. What the buyer wants done. PWS is outcome-based; SOW prescribes how.
11. **BPA** — Blanket Purchase Agreement. Pre-negotiated deal with one agency. 80% of Schedule revenue flows through BPAs, not stand-alone task orders.
12. **IDIQ** — Indefinite Delivery / Indefinite Quantity. A contract vehicle with a ceiling but no guaranteed minimum.
13. **IFF** — Industrial Funding Fee. 0.75% of Schedule sales, remitted to GSA quarterly.
14. **MFC** — Most Favored Customer. Your lowest commercial price — disclosed to GSA.
15. **CSP-1** — Commercial Sales Practices disclosure. The form where you report MFC.
16. **TAA** — Trade Agreements Act. Products must be made in US or designated countries.
17. **SDB / WOSB / SDVOSB / HUBZone / 8(a)** — Small business set-aside programs. Each has separate certification.
18. **CPARS** — Contractor Performance Assessment Reporting System. Your federal report card. A single "Marginal" rating kills future awards.
19. **FAR** — Federal Acquisition Regulation. The rulebook. FAR 8.4 covers Schedule ordering.
20. **COR / KO** — Contracting Officer's Representative / Contracting Officer. KO signs contracts; COR runs them day-to-day. Always copy the KO in writing.

## The trap most small businesses fall into

They learn the top 5 (GSA, SAM, NAICS, RFP, BPA), miss #14 (MFC) and #18 (CPARS), and either lose the contract or their next three re-competes.
