This draft tests whether a contrarian hook on pricing — reframing the question from "what price?" to "what price can I defend?" — drives higher comment engagement than narrative-led posts. Pricing is a high-anxiety topic for the ICP: many have already made these mistakes or fear making them on submission. The numbered format (1-4 items) gives the post a structured, scannable shape that tends to generate saves and reactions on LinkedIn, while the closing question ("What's the pricing mistake you wish someone had flagged?") invites experience-sharing confessionals that typically drive comment volume. Expected score movement: moderate lift in reactions from the "I've been there" recognition effect, and above-average comments because the CTA asks for a specific mistake rather than a general opinion — specificity lowers the barrier to respond.
