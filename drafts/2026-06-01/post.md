Most small businesses ask: "What price should I put on my GSA Schedule?"

The right question is: "What price can I defend — and still win work?"

That distinction matters more than most people realize. I've seen companies get approved with pricing that immediately killed their competitiveness. Nobody warned them before they signed.

Here are four pricing mistakes that silently wreck GSA margins:

1. **Quoting your best commercial rate.** Your Basis of Award price is the floor, not the ceiling. If you routinely discount below it for commercial clients, you've created a compliance problem you'll discover at modification time.

2. **Ignoring the Most Favored Customer rule.** GSA wants parity with your best customer. If you don't know who that is or what you charge them, you're negotiating blind.

3. **Loading in all your overhead upfront.** Contracting Officers will push back. Build in room to negotiate down — not because you'll give it away, but because the CO expects to "win" something.

4. **Setting prices you can't update later.** Economic Price Adjustments exist for a reason. If you don't negotiate EPA terms upfront, you're locked in.

Pricing isn't just a number. It's the architecture of every deal you'll close on Schedule.
