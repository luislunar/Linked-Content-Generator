"Getting certified gets you contracts."

Here's what's actually true.

Certifications open the door. They don't win anything once you're inside. There's a lot more fine print than most people realize before they spend 6 months on an application.

Five things nobody explains upfront:

1. **Certifications expand where you compete — they don't guarantee wins.** SDVOSB or 8(a) status gets you into set-aside competitions. Your pricing, past performance, and proposal quality win the bid. The cert is the entry ticket, not the award.

2. **You can stack certifications.** WOSB and 8(a) aren't mutually exclusive. Many companies hold both — different set-aside pools, same legal entity. More certifications means more doors, not more complexity.

3. **The 8(a) program has a 9-year clock.** When you enter determines how much runway you actually get. Companies that wait until late in their eligibility window end up with 2–3 years of benefit instead of the full term. Timing your application is a strategic decision.

4. **SDVOSB for VA set-asides requires CVE verification — not just SAM registration.** This is the step companies skip. Bidding on VA set-asides without CVE verification isn't a technicality. It's a misrepresentation risk.

5. **HUBZone isn't a box you check once.** Your employees and principal office must remain within the zone throughout contract performance. Companies lose HUBZone certification mid-contract every year because they didn't realize the compliance requirements are ongoing.

The certifications are tools. The strategy around them is what actually moves the needle.
