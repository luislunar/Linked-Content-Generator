Your SAM.gov registration expired while you were busy running your business.

No dramatic story. Just a quiet lapse that locks you out of every active contract until it's resolved — and the agency has no obligation to wait.

I've seen this happen to companies with three years of federal experience. One renewal skipped, one profile outdated, and suddenly they're disqualified during a recompete cycle.

Five things your SAM profile needs right now:

1. **Renewal date on the calendar** — SAM expires 365 days from your last renewal, not the calendar year. Set a 60-day alert.
2. **NAICS codes review** — Stale codes make you invisible to contracting officers searching for exactly what you offer.
3. **Validation buffer built in** — Entity re-validation takes 7–10 business days. Never wait until you have an active RFQ in hand.
4. **Banking details confirmed** — Awards route to the bank on file. Not the account you mentioned to your CO in an email.
5. **Points of contact updated** — The government contacts whoever is listed. If that person left your company, you're missing notices right now.

If you haven't logged into SAM.gov in the last 90 days, open a tab today.
