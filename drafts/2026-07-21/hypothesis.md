This draft tests the `alert` hook pattern on a `sam` topic delivered as a `tips_list`. The core bet is that an urgency-framed opener ("your registration expired while you were busy") hits harder than an educational framing because it forces the reader to self-check immediately rather than file it as general knowledge. The numbered checklist format (1–5) is chosen over `→` bullets to signal a structured audit rather than a loose observation list, which should increase perceived actionability. The community question ("when did you last do a full review — what did you find?") is designed to surface real experiences: readers who caught an expired registration or wrong NAICS code have a specific story to tell, which drives comments more than a generic opinion prompt. Expected outcome: above-average reactions from the "oh I should check mine" recognition effect, and meaningful comments from people who have a specific SAM horror story or surprise.
