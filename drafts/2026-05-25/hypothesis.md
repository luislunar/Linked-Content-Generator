This draft tests the `truth` hook pattern on the `myths` topic in a `carousel` format — the first carousel in the current 7-day window. The hypothesis is that a 3-myth framework presented as swipeable slides outperforms a single-post myth-bust on engagement because the slide format creates a "swipe to reveal" pull: each myth landing builds cumulative tension, and the close slide's direct question ("which one held you back?") invites personal disclosure at the moment of highest emotional investment. The caption uses a credibility-first `truth` opener ("nobody warns you…") rather than a list hook, which should lower the friction for lurkers who nearly abandoned GovCon over one of these misconceptions. Expected movements: comments (experience-share prompt resonates with anyone who was stopped by a false belief) and reactions (myth validation delivers a recognition hit — "I thought that too").
