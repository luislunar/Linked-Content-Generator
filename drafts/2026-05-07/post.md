"Should I be a prime or a sub?"

Wrong question. Here's the right one:

"What capability does this team need that I'm uniquely positioned to fill?"

That reframe changes how you pursue teaming deals entirely.

Wrong approach → Right approach:

→ Cold-pitch large primes → Target recompetes where the incumbent needs your cert (8(a), WOSB, SDVOSB, HUBZone)
→ Sign any teaming agreement fast → Negotiate scope, labor categories, and revenue share before the proposal drops
→ Lead with all your NAICS codes → Lead with the 2 you have proven past performance in
→ Sub indefinitely and wait → Sub for 2–3 years, build past performance, then prime your own IDIQ

One thing most people miss: a teaming agreement without a defined scope of work is a promise, not a partnership. And promises don't win proposals.

The prime/sub line isn't permanent. Start where you are. Build what you need. Flip it.
