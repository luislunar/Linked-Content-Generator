I got a call on a Tuesday morning.

"Josh, my CO just told me my company is off the contract. What happened?"

I pulled up her SAM.gov profile. Registration expired. Thirty-seven days ago.

She'd been in business for 11 years. Selling to federal agencies for 6 of them. This wasn't carelessness — she just didn't know SAM.gov auto-expiration was a real thing with real consequences. No one had ever told her. And SAM.gov certainly didn't warn her.

Here's the part that still gets me: the contract was for services she was already delivering. Work in progress. Payment already being processed. Then it stopped — because a form hadn't been updated on a government portal most people forget exists.

SAM.gov doesn't send reminders the way your bank does. It expires quietly. Annually, without fail. And when it does, you don't just lose access to new opportunities — active payments and contracts can be impacted immediately.

The fix took less than a day. The disruption took weeks to unwind.

If you have any active federal business, check your SAM.gov expiration date today. Not this week. Today. It's sitting in your SAM profile under "Registration Expiration Date" — and it might be closer than you think.

What's the closest you've come to a SAM.gov lapse? Or have you already lived this? Be honest. 😅
