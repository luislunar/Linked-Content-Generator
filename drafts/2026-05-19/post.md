Before you apply for 8(a), WOSB, or SDVOSB — read this first.

These certifications don't get you contracts. They get you a seat at a different table.

→ 8(a) is the most powerful set-aside in small business GovCon. The SBA can sole-source up to $4.5M (services) or $7M (manufacturing) with no competition. You get 9 years — the clock starts at approval.
→ WOSB and EDWOSB unlock set-asides across NAICS codes where women-owned firms are underrepresented. Self-certification is accepted on most contracts — SBA certification adds credibility but isn't required.
→ SDVOSB stacks with other certifications. Hold a GSA Schedule AND SDVOSB status? You're among the most competitive vendors for VA contracts.
→ HUBZone is geography-first. Your principal office and 35% of employees must be in a designated zone. The map changes — verify your eligibility before you apply.
→ None of these replace past performance, pricing strategy, or knowing which agencies actually use set-asides for your NAICS codes.

Getting certified opens a door. Knowing which door leads somewhere is the part most businesses skip.
