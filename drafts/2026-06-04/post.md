He had the GSA Schedule for 14 months and had never won a federal contract.

When he came to us, he wasn't frustrated with the Schedule itself — he was frustrated because he didn't know what to do next. The paperwork was done. The contract was approved. But there was no system for finding work, no way to know when an agency actually needed what he sold, no relationship with a single contracting officer.

We helped him build that system. He started responding to Sources Sought notices — market research requests from agencies before RFPs even open — to introduce his company before competition started. He put industry days on the calendar in his top two NAICS codes. He built a watch list of three active recompetes expiring in his space within 18 months.

Eight months later: first task order awarded. $47K. Small, but the agency called him back. The recompete that followed was $190K. He now has two more bids in flight.

The Schedule is access to the federal marketplace. That's all it is. It does not come with a customer list, a pipeline, or a roadmap for winning. Building those is a completely separate skill, and most GSA holders never develop it.
