Month 10 of chasing my first GSA contract, I was convinced Schedule was a scam.

We'd been awarded 3 months earlier.
Uploaded to GSA Advantage.
Attended 2 APEX events.
Sent 47 cold emails.

2 replies. Both "keep us posted."

Month 12 — an agency we'd emailed in month 9 dropped an RFQ on eBuy. We won. $94K.

Not life-changing. But it put us on the CPARS board.

Over-delivered. Got an "Exceptional" rating.

Month 18 — $847K BPA call order. The COR told me they'd picked us over 3 Schedule holders because of the CPARS rating. Not the proposal. Not the price. The rating from a $94K job four months earlier.

The lessons, honestly:

→ The silence in months 8–11 *is* the job. Cold outreach is the work.
→ Your first award is a footprint, not a revenue event. Compete for the small one.
→ CPARS compounds more than marketing does.
→ Saying no to the CO in negotiation is allowed. Hold your CSP-1 number.
