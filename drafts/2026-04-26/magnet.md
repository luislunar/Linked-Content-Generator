# How I Won My First $847K GSA Contract (A Real Post-Mortem)

Names and specifics are composite/anonymized; the numbers and sequence are real. This is what actually happens between "submit proposal" and "first deposit hits the account" — told honestly, because the LinkedIn version everyone posts skips the 4 months of silence in the middle.

## The setup

- Small IT services firm, 8 employees, ~$1.8M commercial revenue
- No federal experience
- Decided to pursue GSA Schedule in January
- First big award: **$847K BPA call order**, 18 months later

## Month 0 — The decision

Commercial pipeline was tightening. Three of our five largest clients were private-equity-backed and cutting vendor budgets simultaneously. We needed a revenue diversifier that wasn't correlated with VC cycles.

## Months 1–3 — Pre-application

- 2 weeks reconstructing 2 years of invoices for the MFC disclosure (pulled from QuickBooks + email)
- 1 week picking the right SIN (got it wrong the first time, pivoted)
- 3 weeks drafting labor categories. The hardest part: convincing myself our "Senior Consultant" title actually mapped to a federal labor category description. It did.

## Months 4–5 — Proposal drafting

Submitted late on a Friday in month 5. I felt great for 11 days. Then the CO's clarification request arrived.

## Months 6–7 — Negotiation

The CO asked for a deeper MFC discount than we'd proposed. We held at CSP-1 backup (+6%, not the +12% he wanted). This is the single most important moment in the whole process. **Saying no to the CO is allowed.** It's not a relationship — it's a negotiation.

Contract awarded end of month 7.

## Months 8–11 — The silence

This is the part nobody posts about.

We uploaded to GSA Advantage. We attended two APEX Accelerator events. We sent 47 cold emails. We got 2 replies. Both were "interesting, keep us posted."

By month 10, I was convinced Schedule was a scam.

## Month 12 — The break

An agency we'd cold-emailed in month 9 posted an RFQ on eBuy. We responded in 36 hours. We won.

The order was $94K. Not life-changing. But it put us on the CPARS board.

## Months 13–17 — Compounding

We over-delivered. Got an "Exceptional" CPARS rating. That rating got us shortlisted for two BPAs we didn't even apply to — the CO pulled our info from CPARS directly.

## Month 18 — $847K

A BPA call order for an 18-month engagement. The COR told me later they'd compared three Schedule holders and picked us because of the CPARS rating. Not the proposal. Not the price. The rating from a $94K job four months earlier.

## What I'd tell past me

1. **The silence in months 8–11 is the job, not a problem.** Cold outreach *is* the work during this period. Don't stop.
2. **Your first award is a footprint, not a revenue event.** Compete for the small one — the $94K gets you the $847K.
3. **CPARS is everything.** Treat the $94K job like it's $8.4M in future pipeline. Because it is.
4. **Saying no to the CO in negotiation is allowed.** Hold your CSP-1 number.
