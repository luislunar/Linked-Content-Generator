Nobody talks enough about what happens after your GSA Schedule gets approved.

Most small businesses treat approval as the finish line. It's not. It's the starting line.

Here's your post-approval pipeline checklist:

1. **Set SAM.gov keyword alerts** → filter by your NAICS codes so you're notified the moment an agency posts an RFP in your space
2. **Respond to Sources Sought notices** → these pre-RFP market research posts are your chance to get on a contracting officer's radar before competition officially opens
3. **Mine USASpending.gov** → find out which agencies buy what you sell, at what volume, and who they're currently buying from
4. **Read agency procurement forecasts** → published annually, these list planned acquisitions — most GSA Schedule holders never look at them
5. **Reach out to contracting officers directly** → but only after you've done steps 1–4. Generic outreach wastes everyone's time.

Getting on the GSA Schedule opens the door. Doing this work is how you walk through it.
