This draft tests the `truth` hook pattern ("Nobody talks enough about...") on the `pipeline` topic using a `checklist` format.

The hook targets a pain point that's largely invisible until someone has already gone through Schedule approval: most small businesses optimize for getting the Schedule but have no systematic approach to finding buyers once they have it. This creates a knowledge gap that Josh can credibly fill with direct experience. The checklist format was chosen because pipeline activity is inherently sequential — each step builds on the previous one — and numbered steps make the post immediately actionable (readers can check off what they're already doing). The ICP (small business owners who've gotten the Schedule or are close to it) will recognize themselves in the "finish line" framing and feel the tension before the pivot. Expected impact: reactions should lift because the uncomfortable truth creates an "I never thought about that" moment; comments should lift strongly because the closing question is specific enough to invite real experience-sharing (readers will say which steps they skip and why). This post does not test an experiment variable.
