# The 5-Minute GSA Readiness Check

A quick self-assessment for small businesses wondering if they qualify for a GSA Schedule contract. Takes 5 minutes. No emails, no gatekeeping.

---

## Why this matters

The federal government spends **$50M+ per day** through GSA Schedules. Most of it goes to contractors who already have one. The #1 reason small businesses skip this channel isn't that they don't qualify — it's that they assume they don't.

This sheet exists to settle that in 5 minutes.

---

## The 7 checks

### ✅ Check 1 — Two years of operating history
Have you been in business for at least 2 years? (Newer companies can still get there — see workaround at the end.)

### ✅ Check 2 — Past performance
Can you show 3+ invoices or case studies from commercial customers in the last 24 months?

### ✅ Check 3 — Commercial pricing
Do you have a published price list, rate card, or standard SKUs? GSA negotiates against your commercial pricing — you need one.

### ✅ Check 4 — Financial stability
Can you produce a balance sheet + P&L that shows positive operations or a clean runway? A Dun & Bradstreet D-U-N-S number helps.

### ✅ Check 5 — SAM.gov registration
Are you registered at SAM.gov? (Free, ~60 min — required before anything else moves.)

### ✅ Check 6 — A product or service GSA buys
Do your offerings fall under an existing GSA schedule category? (Top categories: IT, professional services, facilities, furniture, office supplies.)

### ✅ Check 7 — Bandwidth for a 6-month process
Do you have someone internal (or a consultant) who can spend ~4 hrs/week for 4–6 months responding to government questions?

---

## How to read your score

- **6–7 checked:** you're ready. Start your Schedule application this month.
- **4–5 checked:** you're close. Fix the gaps in the next 30 days.
- **3 or fewer:** don't start yet. Build the missing pieces first — applying early wastes 100+ hours.

---

## The workaround for "only 1 year in business"

You can partner with a firm that has 2+ years of history and submit as a team. This is common, legal, and faster than waiting.
