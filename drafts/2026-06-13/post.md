Before you frame that 8(a) certificate — do you know what happens after the approval letter arrives?

The federal government awards roughly $20 billion annually through the 8(a) program alone. WOSB, SDVOSB, and HUBZone set-asides add tens of billions more. These programs exist because Congress wanted small and disadvantaged businesses to have a real shot at federal revenue.

Here's what they didn't put in the program guide: the certification unlocks access. It doesn't generate pipeline.

I've talked to dozens of 8(a) companies six months post-certification with zero federal revenue. They got the certificate. Registered on SAM.gov. Built a capability statement. And then they waited.

Certifications work when you pair them with an active BD strategy — responding to sources sought, building agency relationships, teaming with primes who need your certification to hit their small business subcontracting goals. The cert is a door. You still have to knock.

A certification is the beginning of the work, not the finish line.
