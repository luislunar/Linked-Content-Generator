Two years on the GSA Schedule. Zero awards. This wasn't bad luck.

A professional services company came to us after spending two years with an active GSA Schedule and nothing to show for it. They'd submitted on RFQs. They had the right NAICS codes. They even had a decent capability statement.

The problem was buried in the contract they'd signed.

When we audited their pricing, we found their rates were sitting 18–23% above market for their SIN category. They'd negotiated the Schedule themselves — without understanding how Basis of Award pricing works, and without knowing what competitors were charging on comparable Schedules.

Contracting officers can see your prices. Buyers compare. When your rates are 20% above the next qualified vendor, they move on without saying a word. You never find out why you lost.

We helped them renegotiate their pricing structure, brought them in line with market rates, and within eight months they had their first federal award.

The Schedule doesn't sell. But mispriced rates guarantee it never will.
