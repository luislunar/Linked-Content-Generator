Wrong question: "What should I charge on my GSA Schedule?"

Here's the right one: "What price can I document, defend in a post-award audit, and still win federal work with?"

Most small businesses price their GSA Schedule the same way they price commercial work — then they submit and realize they've negotiated themselves into a corner.

Your Basis of Award (the commercial price that justifies your GSA rate) has to be airtight before you file. Volume discounts, relationship deals, one-time exceptions — GSA will find every price that contradicts your stated commercial baseline. When they do, you lose negotiating leverage fast. And once the rate is locked, it's locked.

Underprice yourself and you're stuck for years. Overprice and you won't win awards. The margin for error is smaller than most people expect.

Build the pricing story before you submit. Not after.
