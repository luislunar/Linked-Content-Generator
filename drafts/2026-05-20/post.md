Most small businesses lose their GSA pricing negotiation before the first meeting.

Not because their rates are wrong. Because they can't explain them.

GSA Contracting Officers don't automatically push you to your lowest price — they test whether your pricing is commercially defensible. That test is the Basis of Award: the documentation behind your Schedule rates. Your commercial invoices. Your discount history. Who your most-favored customer is and what rate they actually received.

Come in with a number but no paper trail, and the CO negotiates you down. Come in with 12 months of documented commercial invoices and clear conditions around every discount you've given, and you have a defensible position — and you can hold it.

The mistake I see most often: businesses price based on what they hope the government will pay, not what they've actually charged in the commercial market. Those are two very different conversations at the negotiating table.

Build your commercial pricing paper trail before you submit. Know your most-favored customer rate. Know every discount you've given in the last year and why you gave it.
