"Should I go for GSA Schedule or SEWP or CIO-SP4?"

Wrong question.

Small businesses treat "federal contracting" as one thing. It's a menu of vehicles — each with different access, competition levels, and ceiling sizes.

Pick wrong and you compete against the wrong opponents.

Fast decision tree:

→ First $10M in federal revenue: GSA MAS only.
→ $10M–$50M: GSA MAS + one agency-specific GWAC (SEWP if IT, CIO-SP4 if health IT).
→ $50M+: Full portfolio. You'll know by then.

The 3 traps I see small businesses fall into:

1. Going for CIO-SP4 or SEWP first. These are $20B–$50B GWACs with 5-year cycles and 100+ incumbent vendors. If you're pre-revenue in federal, you won't win. Start with MAS.

2. Thinking SAM.gov registration IS the contract vehicle. SAM is a directory. Agencies buy off vehicles — MAS, SEWP, OASIS+ — not off SAM. You need both.

3. Pursuing 3 vehicles simultaneously. Each has its own proposal effort + compliance overhead. One vehicle done right beats three half-done.
