# GSA vs. SAM vs. SEWP vs. CIO-SP4: Which Vehicle Fits?

Small businesses often treat "federal contracting" as one thing. It's not — it's a menu of contract vehicles, each with different access, competition levels, and ceiling sizes. Pick wrong and you compete against the wrong opponents.

## Quick decision tree

```
Do you sell to the federal government at all?
  ├─ No → you need to be in SAM.gov first (SAM is not a vehicle — it's registration)
  └─ Yes ↓

What's your primary offering?
  ├─ IT products/services  → evaluate GSA MAS IT SIN, SEWP VI (NASA), CIO-SP4 (NIH/HHS)
  ├─ Professional services → GSA MAS Professional Services, OASIS+ (GSA)
  ├─ Commercial products   → GSA MAS (correct SIN), agency BPAs
  └─ Construction          → MATOC / SABER (DoD), GSA 00CORP
```

## Side-by-side

| Vehicle | Owner | Scope | Ceiling | Award timeline | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **SAM.gov** | Federal-wide | Registration, not a vehicle | N/A | Days | Required for everything below |
| **GSA MAS** | GSA | Broad (all civilian + most DoD) | Unlimited | 4–9 months | Most small businesses starting out |
| **SEWP VI** | NASA (gov-wide IT) | IT products/services | $20B | 18+ months, closed cycles | IT vendors with $5M+ revenue |
| **CIO-SP4** | NIH/HHS | IT + health IT | $50B | Closed; recompete TBD | IT primes with federal past perf |
| **OASIS+** | GSA | Professional services | Unlimited | 12–18 months | Services firms ready for on-ramps |
| **8(a) STARS III** | SBA | IT services, 8(a) set-aside | $50B | By 8(a) cycle | 8(a)-certified IT firms |

## The 3 traps

**Trap 1 — Going for CIO-SP4 or SEWP first.** These are massive GWACs with 5-year cycles and 100s of vendors. If you're pre-revenue in federal, you won't win. **Start with GSA MAS.**

**Trap 2 — Thinking SAM.gov registration = contract vehicle.** SAM is a directory. Agencies don't "buy off SAM" — they buy off vehicles (MAS, SEWP, etc). You need both.

**Trap 3 — Pursuing multiple vehicles simultaneously.** Each requires proposal effort + compliance maintenance. One vehicle, done right, beats three half-done.

## The rule of thumb

**First $10M in federal revenue:** GSA MAS only.
**$10M–$50M:** GSA MAS + one agency-specific GWAC (SEWP or CIO-SP4 if IT).
**$50M+:** Full portfolio — but you'll know by then.
