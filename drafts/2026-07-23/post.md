"We need past performance before we can compete." I hear this every single week.

Wrong premise entirely.

Teaming agreements exist precisely so you don't have to spend years building a federal track record before your first award. A well-structured teaming agreement lets you bring your technical depth to the table while your prime partner brings the past performance record. The contracting officer sees one qualified team — not a rookie and a veteran duct-taped together.

I've watched companies with zero federal history land their first contract in under 12 months. Not because they got lucky. Because they found the right prime, structured the agreement correctly, and showed up to market as a unified team — not as a solo act waiting for permission to exist.

The barrier isn't your past performance history. It's your reluctance to pick up the phone.

What's stopped you from reaching out to a potential teaming partner? Be honest.
