# 10 GSA Application Mistakes That Cost Small Businesses $50K+

Every rejected GSA Schedule proposal costs 4–6 months of lost revenue. These are the 10 mistakes I see in ~80% of the proposals I teardown — ranked by how expensive they are to fix after submission.

## The 10 mistakes

### 1. Submitting before 2 years of past-performance data
GSA requires **2 years of relevant commercial revenue** on the products/services you're proposing. Submit earlier → automatic rejection. Cost: 6+ months waiting to reapply.

### 2. Missing CPARS or past-performance references
You need **3–5 verifiable references** with contact info. "References available on request" = rejected. Cost: 2 months re-gathering.

### 3. Not completing SAM.gov registration properly
UEI missing, NAICS codes wrong, reps & certs expired → proposal never reaches the contracting officer. Cost: 3 weeks.

### 4. Incorrect Most Favored Customer (MFC) disclosure
You must disclose your **lowest commercial price** in the last 12 months. Hiding it = fraud. Disclosing wrong = contract termination later. Cost: up to the full contract value.

### 5. Pricing without Commercial Sales Practices (CSP-1) backup
You need invoices/contracts proving your MFC claim. No backup = rejection. Cost: 1 month gathering invoices.

### 6. Wrong Schedule / SIN selection
Putting IT services under a Professional Services SIN = rejection. The wrong SIN also means zero discoverability on GSA Advantage. Cost: full resubmission.

### 7. Labor category descriptions that don't match commercial
Your GSA labor categories (e.g., "Senior Consultant") must map to your commercial roles with identical responsibilities. Mismatched descriptions = rejected or price-negotiated down 15%+.

### 8. Ignoring the Trade Agreements Act (TAA)
Products must be manufactured in the US or a TAA-designated country. One non-compliant SKU kills the whole Schedule. Cost: full offering restructure.

### 9. No marketing strategy in Section M
GSA wants to see how you'll actually win task orders. "We will market our services" = rejected. Need: named target agencies, BPA strategy, teaming partners.

### 10. Submitting a static proposal (no GSA Advantage catalog prep)
You need your catalog ready to upload within 30 days of award. Unprepared = contract suspended. Cost: risk of Schedule cancellation.
