This draft tests a question hook (first time in 3 days after consecutive statement openers) combined with the carousel format, which hasn't run in 7+ days. The myths topic was chosen because it directly addresses the ICP's core emotional blockers — past performance anxiety, feeling too small to compete, and fear of losing on price — all three are things prospects tell Josh regularly and that prevent them from starting the application process at all.

The carousel format creates a natural multi-step engagement loop: the caption poses a question, the swipe-through delivers resolution, and the close slide converts readers into commenters. Expected impact: reactions should rise because the myth/reality reveal structure has high shareability (people tag peers who are stuck on these same beliefs); comments should see a stronger lift than average because the close question ("which one almost stopped you?") is a direct experience-share prompt with low friction — it only asks for one myth, not a long answer. The question hook in the caption is designed to create a curiosity gap that pulls people into the swipe.
