Nobody talks enough about how certifications can work against you — if you earn them without a strategy.

I've watched businesses spend 12–18 months securing WOSB, SDVOSB, or 8(a) status, then apply it to every opportunity on the board without understanding what each cert actually does for them.

Five things most small businesses get wrong about set-aside certifications:

1. **8(a) is a 9-year program.** Your graduation date is set the day you're approved. If you don't map your revenue plan around that exit, you'll be starting over when the protection disappears.

2. **HUBZone has a live address requirement.** Your principal office and at least 35% of your employees must reside in a designated HUBZone area. Moving — even to a better office — can invalidate your certification.

3. **SDVOSB involves two separate processes.** VA verification and SBA self-certification are distinct. Some businesses complete one and assume they're covered. They're not.

4. **WOSB restricts competition. It doesn't win contracts.** Agencies still score capability statements, past performance, and pricing the same way. The cert narrows the field; your proposal has to close it.

5. **Stacking certifications is legal and strategic.** A business holding WOSB + HUBZone + 8(a) can pursue four different set-aside types. Most businesses never check which combinations they qualify for.

The certification is the door. Your strategy is the key.
