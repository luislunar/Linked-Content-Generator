"You don't need past performance to win federal contracts."

I've heard this so many times I stopped counting.

Here's what's actually true.

You don't need a 10-year track record. You need the right entry point.

→ **Commercial experience counts.** GSA evaluators accept private-sector work as relevant past performance. If you've delivered the same service commercially, document it.
→ **Subcontracting builds the record.** Get in as a sub on one federal award. One performance evaluation from a prime changes your entire competitive position.
→ **Simplified acquisitions exist for a reason.** Contracts under $250K have relaxed past performance requirements. That's the on-ramp.
→ **Key personnel experience substitutes.** If your team has federal delivery experience, even under a different employer, it counts toward your proposal.
→ **Teaming fills the gap.** Partner with an incumbent who has the past performance you lack. Agencies evaluate the team, not just the prime.

The myth isn't that past performance matters. It does.

The myth is that you have to already have it before you can get it.
