This draft tests whether a myth-bust carousel on pricing can generate higher comment volume than recent single-card formats. Pricing is one of the highest-anxiety topics for GSA applicants — they often don't know what they don't know, which creates strong identification with the myths.

The three myths chosen (copy-paste pricing, race to zero, price lock) surface real misconceptions that appear repeatedly in discovery calls. Each has a sharp, counterintuitive reality that rewards reading all the way through.

The carousel format (5 slides: cover + 3 myth/reality pairs + close) forces longer dwell time than single cards, which should lift impressions and reach. The close slide's community question — "drop the pricing myth you believed when you first started" — is personal and specific enough to surface real stories rather than one-word reactions.

Expected lift: reactions and comments vs. the last three posts (inspiration/personal_story/hot_take), where single-card formats averaged fewer than 5 comments. The myth-bust hook pattern also hasn't been used recently, adding freshness to the feed.
