This draft tests a **question hook** on a **myths** topic using the `inspiration` format — a short, punchy prose post with no bullets. The core belief being punctured is one of the most persistent myths in GovCon: that earning a GSA Schedule is itself the win, rather than the starting gate.

The hook ("Before you celebrate...") creates a pattern interrupt for anyone currently in the application process or freshly approved — exactly the ICP moment where this belief is most dangerous. The contrast between "the paperwork was the easy part" and the implied reality of active BD is designed to provoke reactions from both camps: those who lived it and want to validate the truth, and those who are about to make the same mistake and feel seen.

Expected outcome: high **reactions** from Schedule holders who identify with the "waited for the phone to ring" experience, and strong **comment pull** from the closing community question — it asks for a specific, personal revelation (what nobody warned you about), which tends to outperform generic opinion questions. The short format (~124 words) is well below the recent average, which should help stop-the-scroll performance in the feed.
