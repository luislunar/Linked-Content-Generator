A certification is not a contract.

I know that sounds blunt. But I've watched too many small businesses treat getting certified as the finish line.

8(a), WOSB, SDVOSB, HUBZone — these are tools. What you do with them determines whether they generate revenue.

→ **Identify agencies that set aside contracts in your category.** Not all agencies use all certifications equally. Check USASpending.gov — filter by set-aside type and NAICS code. Go where the money already flows.
→ **Get on contract vehicles that recognize your cert.** A WOSB certification with a GSA Schedule and an 8(a) STARS III sub slot is a different competitive position than a WOSB cert alone.
→ **Build relationships before solicitations drop.** Contracting officers want to know who you are before they write your cert into a set-aside. Sources sought responses, capability briefings, industry days — that's how you get seen.
→ **Look for sole-source thresholds.** 8(a) sole-source authority goes up to $4.5M for services. You don't need to compete. You need a CO who knows you exist.

The certification gets you in the room.

Your strategy keeps you there.
