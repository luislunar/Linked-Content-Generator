A manufacturing firm almost lost their first federal recompete last quarter. Not because a stronger competitor showed up. Because SAM.gov expired while nobody was watching — and nobody caught it until a Contracting Officer called with four words: "Your registration is lapsed." By then, the recompete window was two weeks away.

SAM.gov doesn't send a bold warning. It doesn't call. It doesn't send a certified letter. Your registration simply expires — and when it does, payments freeze, awards can't legally process, and a CO has no legal mechanism to continue the relationship, no matter how strong the past performance is. This client had 11 months of solid work behind them. None of it mattered until the SAM issue was resolved.

We renewed their SAM registration within 72 hours, worked with the CO to document the lapse, and had them back on the approved vendor list before the recompete window closed. They kept the contract. It was closer than it had any right to be. The fix was straightforward. The near-miss was entirely avoidable.

When your SAM registration expires — not if — what's your recovery plan? Do you have it written down somewhere, or is it still "I'll figure it out when it happens"?
