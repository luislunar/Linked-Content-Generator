This draft tests an **alert hook + numbered checklist format** on a **compliance** topic targeting active GSA Schedule holders — a segment that follows Josh precisely because they have a contract and are navigating post-award obligations.

The hook ("Your GSA Schedule is active. That doesn't mean your compliance is.") creates tension by implying a gap between status and safety. Active holders will self-identify with the concern immediately; prospective holders will file it as future preparation. Both audiences have reason to read on.

The 5-item numbered format (no `→` bullets) provides clean visual contrast against the last three posts, which used story prose, prose, and comparison respectively. The `single-06-checklist` dark-navy card continues the alert aesthetic and differentiates from yesterday's cream `single-05-beforeafter`.

Expected engagement lift: **reactions** from active holders who recognize at least one item they've overlooked (the TAA sourcing and CAV audit points are particularly under-discussed); **comments** from the community question, which asks for a specific experience rather than a generic opinion. The fill-in-the-blank structure of the CTA ("what do you wish you'd known before you got your award") makes it easy to respond with a concrete answer.
