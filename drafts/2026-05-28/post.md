Your GSA Schedule is active. That doesn't mean your compliance is.

Here are 5 things that catch contract holders off guard:

1. **Quarterly sales reporting** — You're required to report every dollar of GSA sales each quarter through the Vendor Portal. Miss it, and GSA flags your contract for review.

2. **Price list currency** — When your commercial rates change, your GSA price list must reflect that too. Charging above your awarded rate is a FAR violation — even if it happened by accident.

3. **TAA product sourcing** — The Trade Agreements Act restricts which countries your products can originate from. A lot of small businesses never verify this until an audit surfaces the problem.

4. **Contractor Assessment Visits** — GSA can review your records at any time: pricing basis, sales data, customer eligibility. "I didn't know" is not a compliance defense.

5. **5-year option periods** — Your Schedule has built-in option periods. Miss the renewal window and the contract lapses — and you restart the full application process from scratch.

Getting on the Schedule is the milestone. Staying compliant is the discipline.
