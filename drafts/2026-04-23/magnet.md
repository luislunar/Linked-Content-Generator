# The 6-Month GSA Contract Playbook (Week-by-Week)

Most small businesses treat "getting on Schedule" as the finish line. It's not — it's week 12 of a 26-week journey to your first task order. Here's the week-by-week plan that's taken 40+ of my clients from zero to their first award.

## Phase 1 — Pre-application (Weeks 1–4)

**Week 1:** SAM.gov registration (UEI, CAGE, NAICS, reps & certs). Budget 3–5 business days for CAGE assignment.
**Week 2:** Pull 2 years of commercial invoices. Identify Most Favored Customer (MFC). Document the 3–5 past-performance references you'll cite.
**Week 3:** Select the correct SIN(s). Validate pricing against GSA Advantage (40th–60th percentile target).
**Week 4:** Draft labor category descriptions + product catalog. TAA compliance review of every SKU.

## Phase 2 — Proposal (Weeks 5–8)

**Week 5:** Draft Section I (technical): past performance, quality controls, production capability.
**Week 6:** Draft Section II (pricing): CSP-1, MFC disclosure, proposed pricelist.
**Week 7:** Draft Section III (marketing): target agencies, BPA strategy, teaming partners.
**Week 8:** Internal red-team review. Submit via eOffer.

## Phase 3 — Negotiation (Weeks 9–12)

**Week 9–10:** Contracting officer assigned. Expect clarification requests within 14 days.
**Week 11:** Negotiation call. CO will push for deeper MFC discount — hold at your CSP-1 number.
**Week 12:** Final Proposal Revision (FPR). Award letter within 5 business days.

## Phase 4 — Activation (Weeks 13–16)

**Week 13:** Upload catalog to GSA Advantage within 30 days (mandatory).
**Week 14:** Register for eBuy. Set up BPA outreach list (top 20 target agencies).
**Week 15:** LinkedIn + website update: "GSA Schedule Contract #XX-XXX-XXXX." Federal buyers search by contract number.
**Week 16:** Attend first APEX Accelerator (formerly PTAC) event in your state. Free, high-leverage.

## Phase 5 — First award (Weeks 17–26)

**Week 17–20:** Cold outreach to 20 target agencies. Goal: 5 discovery calls.
**Week 21–23:** Respond to 2–3 RFQs via eBuy. First RFQ response sets your template.
**Week 24:** First BPA call order awarded (median timeline).
**Week 25:** Deliver. Invoice. Remit IFF.
**Week 26:** First CPARS evaluation submitted. Push for "Exceptional" rating to compound future awards.

## The shortcut most people miss

Week 15 — putting your contract number on LinkedIn and your website. Buyers search for it. Small businesses with visible contract numbers get 3x more RFQ invitations in their first 6 months.
