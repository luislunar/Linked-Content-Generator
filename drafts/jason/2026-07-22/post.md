Most vehicle problems I see aren't "we couldn't win." They're "you were knocking on the wrong door."

I talked with an IT consultant last quarter. SAM was current. Business was real. They'd been testing SEWP V for seven months — made sense on paper. But their core offering sat squarely in a GSA MAS SIN. They had no established relationships with SEWP primes, no past performance in that lane. Every door they knocked on was the wrong one.

We ran a catalog assessment. Moved them to the correct SIN. Refreshed their capability statement for that market. Three inbound RFQs in five months.

The market didn't change. The door did.

Here's the pattern I see most:

→ SEWP V — right for IT products and solutions, but cold outreach without prime relationships burns time.
→ CIO-SP4 — complex program management; if you're under $25M in revenue, this isn't your first move.
→ OASIS+ — large, complex professional services; not the right place to build your first past-performance record.
→ GSA MAS — broadest entry point for small businesses, multiple SINs, lowest barrier to first award.

Vehicle selection isn't inspiration. It's logistics. Pick the wrong one and you spend real time in the wrong aisle.

Which vehicle is your business currently pursuing? Is it actually the right fit for where you are now?
