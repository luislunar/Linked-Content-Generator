Most certifications I see on company profiles? Earning zero federal revenue.

Not because the cert is wrong — because the strategy behind it was.

Here's what I see across the calls I run every week:

→ **8(a):** Sole-source awards up to $4.5M in services. 9-year program. Highest ceiling in the set-aside world — but tight eligibility requirements knock most applicants out before they apply.

→ **WOSB:** Powerful — but only in NAICS codes where the program is authorized. I hear "I have WOSB" more often than I hear it actually driving pipeline. Know your codes before you apply.

→ **SDVOSB:** Real pull at VA and DoD. If those agencies are in your target market, this one earns its keep. Most companies also underestimate how often they can sub to SDVOSB prime holders.

→ **HUBZone:** Underused and geography-locked. The eligible zone map updates annually. Check your office address against the current map before you write it off.

→ **None yet:** Pull USASpending.gov for your NAICS code first. See which set-aside categories are actually awarding in your space. That's the only cert math that matters.

A certification is not a pipeline. It's a door. You still have to knock on it.
