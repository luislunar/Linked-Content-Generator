What's the number one reason GSA proposals stall at pricing review?

Usually it's one of these five — and I see them in proposals every week.

→ No commercial price documentation
Your Basis of Award needs a paper trail. Without it, the CO has full discretion over what you get.

→ Rates set above your own commercial pricing
GSA requires Most Favored Customer rates. If you charge a private client less, you're starting in violation before the schedule is even live.

→ No escalation clause
Flat rates lock you into 2020 labor costs through 2031. That's not stability — it's a margin trap.

→ Bundled services with no line-item breakout
If agencies can't price each deliverable separately, they can't issue the order. You're invisible.

→ Skipping the price realism check
Too low = performance risk flag. Too high = no award. Both are losing positions.

Price it wrong on entry and you're either uncompetitive from day one or locked into a 5-year margin problem.
