This draft tests whether a myth-bust framing anchored in specific prospecting data ("30+ calls this year") drives more comment engagement than a generic educational post. The myth chosen — "the Schedule won't pay off for years" — is a high-frequency objection in sales conversations, which means readers are pre-loaded with an opinion and more likely to respond. The three-point breakdown (no agency targets, no sources-sought, wrong pricing approach) transforms the rebuttal from opinion to evidence, which should lift reactions from analytical buyers who appreciate the data angle. Expected score movers: reactions (contrarian take invites agreement/disagreement) and comments (the closing question asks for a personal "what I wish I knew" answer, which is low-friction and high-personality — easier to answer than a technical question).
