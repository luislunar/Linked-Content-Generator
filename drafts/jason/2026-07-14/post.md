"The GSA Schedule won't pay off for years."

I've heard that exact line on 30+ calls this year.

Here's what I actually track: the companies that land their first award within 12 months of approval had one thing in common — they built a pipeline *before* the contract was approved.

The "2–3 year ROI" isn't a Schedule problem. It's a pipeline problem.

Most of the time it traces back to three things:

→ No agency targets identified before applying
→ No sources-sought responses in year one
→ Pricing negotiated to "just get approved" instead of to win awards

The approval process takes 6–12 months. That part's real.

But the wait-and-see approach that follows? That's what creates the multi-year payoff timeline — not the contract itself.

The Schedule is a distribution channel. It performs in proportion to the pipeline work you put behind it.
