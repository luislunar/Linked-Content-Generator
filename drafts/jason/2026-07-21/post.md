"We're too small for federal contracts."

$12.1 billion disagrees.

That's what small businesses collected from just the top 8 federal industries in Q2 2026 — one quarter, not the whole year.

I hear some version of the "too small" objection on almost every prospecting call. Here's what the actual spending data shows:

→ Custom programming shops: $1.2B last quarter
→ Construction small businesses: $1.7B
→ Engineering and R&D firms combined: $2.7B
→ VA, DHS, and USDA each awarded billions to small businesses — not just DoD

The size of the prize is not the problem.

The barrier is positioning, not eligibility. Most companies that "can't win federal contracts" haven't done the paperwork to get on the field. That's not a market problem — that's a process problem.

There is a meaningful difference between "the federal market doesn't want us" and "we haven't set ourselves up to compete yet."

One is a dead end. The other has a next step.
