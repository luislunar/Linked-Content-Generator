Before you hit submit on your SAM.gov registration — do you know what's actually being checked on the other side?

Most companies treat it like a government form. Fill in the boxes, submit, wait. What's really happening: SAM.gov cross-references your EIN with IRS records, validates your entity against your CAGE code history, and routes everything through a manual review queue that runs 3–12 business days. One mismatch between your legal business name and your IRS filings? The whole registration stalls — silently, with no alert to your inbox.

Before we ever open the SAM portal for a client at GSA Focus, we run a pre-flight checklist: legal name audit against IRS records, CAGE code history pull, and prior registration review for any lapsed dates or expired representations. The document that kills more registrations than anything else? The notarized letter confirming business authority. We see it missing constantly. SAM.gov isn't a form — it's a credentialing process. Companies treating it like a form are the ones calling us three weeks later asking why they're still "pending."
