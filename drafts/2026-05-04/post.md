Before you pursue a GSA Schedule, ask this one question.

"Do agencies actually buy what I sell off a Schedule?"

Not all categories move the same volume. Choosing the right SIN (Special Item Number) determines whether your Schedule generates pipeline or collects dust.

→ **IT products and services (SIN 518210C, 54151S):** Highest volume on MAS. Agencies spend billions annually. Competitive, but the opportunity is real.
→ **Professional services (SIN 541611, 541990):** Strong demand from civilian agencies. Management consulting, training, HR — consistent utilization.
→ **Facilities and construction (SIN 236):** Growing. Infrastructure spending is up. Longer sales cycles but larger awards.
→ **Office supplies and products:** High transaction volume but razor-thin margins. Works if you're already selling commercially at scale.
→ **Emerging categories (cybersecurity, AI/ML, cloud):** Fastest-growing segments. Agencies are actively looking. Less incumbent competition if you move now.

The mistake: picking a SIN based on what you sell, not on what agencies are actually buying.

Before you apply, run a USASpending.gov search for your NAICS code. See who's buying, how much, and from which vehicles.

Which SIN are you on — or considering — and have you verified the demand is actually there?
