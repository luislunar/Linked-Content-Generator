Federal buyers get 40–80 cold emails a week.

Yours isn't special.

Unless it follows 5 rules that almost no one selling to government actually uses:

1. Subject line = the buyer's problem (not your service)
2. Under 120 words
3. One specific ask (15-min call, not "discuss opportunities")
4. Contract number in the signature (buyers filter for it)
5. Send Tue–Thu, 7–9 AM buyer's timezone

Apply those 5, and the templates start working.

I put together the 12 cold email templates my clients use to get 12–22% reply rates from federal buyers.

A few openers that have actually landed:

→ "31% task-order cost reduction at [peer agency]"
→ "[Incumbent]'s contract expires [date] — RFQ alternative"
→ "Your FY26 small-biz goal is 2.1% behind — can help"
→ "Q4 obligation capacity on [category]"

Each one leads with a buyer problem. None of them mention "capabilities" or "partnership."
