# 12 Cold Email Templates That Get Federal Buyers to Reply

Federal buyers get 40–80 cold emails a week. Most hit spam. These 12 templates have a 12–22% reply rate across my client base — because they lead with the buyer's actual problem, not your company's capabilities.

## The rules (ignore these and no template works)

1. **Subject line = one buyer problem.** Not your service. ("Reducing TDY burden at DLA" beats "IT Services — [Company]".)
2. **Under 120 words.** Federal buyers read on phones between meetings.
3. **One specific ask.** 15-minute call, not "discuss opportunities."
4. **Contract number in the signature.** GSA Schedule # XX-XXX-XXXX. Buyers filter for this.
5. **Send Tue–Thu, 7–9 AM buyer's timezone.** Mon morning = inbox flood. Fri = ignored.

## The 12 templates

### 1. The benchmark-data opener
> Subject: 31% task-order cost reduction at [peer agency]
> [First name] — we just completed a 12-month BPA at [peer agency] that cut their [X] spend 31%. Open to a 15-min call to share the post-mortem?

### 2. The GWAC-adjacent hook
> Subject: Alternative to your current [GWAC] vendor
> Noticed [agency] uses [GWAC] for [category]. We deliver the same scope on GSA MAS at ~12% lower TCO. Worth a 15-min call?

### 3. The set-aside leverage
> Subject: [WOSB/SDVOSB] set-aside for your Q3 [category] requirement
> Your Q3 forecast lists a [category] requirement. We're a [WOSB/SDVOSB] prime on GSA — your small-business goal + 30-day turnaround. 15 min?

### 4. The recompete positioning
> Subject: [Incumbent]'s contract expires [date] — RFQ alternative
> [Incumbent]'s BPA for [scope] expires in [X months]. We've delivered comparable scope 4 times on Schedule. Worth a brief call before the recompete?

### 5. The quick-turn capability
> Subject: 72-hour task order fulfillment on [category]
> If your team needs [category] and can't wait for a full solicitation, we can deliver under our Schedule with 72-hour mobilization. 15-min overview?

### 6. The small-business goal gap
> Subject: Your FY26 small-biz goal is 2.1% behind — can help
> Per [public data source], [agency] is tracking 2.1% short of its small-business utilization goal. We're a Schedule-holder in [category]. Quick call?

### 7. The FAR 8.4 speed angle
> Subject: FAR 8.4 ordering — 5-day turnaround on [category]
> Your team can place orders under FAR 8.4 directly off our Schedule in 5 business days. Useful for end-of-FY obligations. 15-min walkthrough?

### 8. The past-performance proof
> Subject: Delivered [X] at [peer agency] under similar scope
> We delivered [specific outcome] at [peer agency] under a Schedule task order in [year]. Happy to share the CPARS rating. 15-min call?

### 9. The teaming-partner intro
> Subject: Teaming under [large prime]'s [contract]
> We team with [large prime] under [contract]. If your requirement exceeds our solo capacity, we can still deliver through that vehicle. Brief call?

### 10. The end-of-FY obligation
> Subject: Q4 obligation capacity on [category]
> Most [category] Schedule vendors cap out on Q4 obligations by mid-August. We have capacity through 9/30. 15-min call to pre-position?

### 11. The industry-day follow-up
> Subject: Follow-up from [industry day] on [specific topic]
> You mentioned [specific concern] at [industry day]. We addressed exactly that at [peer agency]. 15-min walkthrough of the approach?

### 12. The warm-intro parlay
> Subject: [Mutual contact] suggested I reach out re: [category]
> [Mutual contact] at [org] suggested our [capability] might fit your [requirement]. 15-min overview if useful?

## How to use these

Pick the 3 templates that match your specific offering. A/B test in waves of 20. Track reply rate — goal is >12%. Anything under 8% means the subject line is wrong, not the body.
