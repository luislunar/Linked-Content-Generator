One quiet SAM.gov mistake is costing small businesses real federal contracts — and most don't know it's happening.

Your registration can be "active" and still be invisible to the buyers you want.

Here's what actually gets you found:

→ NAICS codes that match how agencies *search*, not how you describe yourself. Pick the codes that show up in active solicitations for your service.
→ Capability narrative written in agency keywords. Buyers paste search terms from solicitations into SAM — your bio has to mirror them.
→ Reps & Certs section reviewed every 90 days. One stale cert (especially size status or 889 representation) auto-disqualifies you on quote evaluations.
→ POC email tied to a monitored inbox. Not the founder's personal Gmail. Not a role address nobody checks.
→ Exclusion check pulled the same day you submit a quote. One sub-contractor on the SAM exclusion list quietly kills a whole bid.

Active ≠ findable. Active ≠ compliant. Active just means the timer is still running.

A 30-minute SAM audit done quarterly catches most of this before it costs you a contract.
