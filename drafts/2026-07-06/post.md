Two years of submitting bids. Strong commercial track record. And the same rejection feedback every single time: "insufficient federal past performance."

The catch-22 is real. You can't build past performance without winning. You can't win without past performance. It feels like a locked door.

For one of my clients — an IT services firm, about 12 employees — the unlock wasn't a new bid strategy. It was a teaming agreement.

We helped him identify a mid-size prime that was already delivering similar work to a federal agency. He came in as a sub with a defined scope: a technical capability they were consistently outsourcing. Clear value. Clear terms.

Eleven months after signing that agreement: his first federal task order. $180K. Not huge — but it was on his record. Past performance established.

Six months later, he bid on a comparable contract as the prime. He won it.

Teaming wasn't a shortcut. It was the legitimate path in — one that most small businesses overlook because they're laser-focused on winning alone.

The question most people ask is: "How do I win a federal contract?"

The better question: "Who's already in the room — and what do I bring to that table?"

If you've been sitting on a teaming arrangement but haven't pulled the trigger — what's been the sticking point? Finding the right prime, negotiating the agreement terms, or something else?
