Nobody warns you what happens after you get certified.

Your 8(a) is approved. Your WOSB is on file. Your SDVOSB is verified.

And then... nothing changes.

Not because certifications don't matter — they matter enormously. But most small businesses treat certification as the destination. It's actually the access key.

Here's what you need to pair with every cert:

1. **Get your NAICS codes right in SAM.gov.** Your cert is invisible to buyers searching for set-aside vendors if the codes don't match the work you do.

2. **Pair it with a GSA Schedule.** GSA eBuy set-asides require a Schedule holder. No Schedule means you're locked out of the easiest RFQs before the competition even starts.

3. **Rewrite your capability statement around your cert.** "8(a) certified" as a lone bullet is table stakes. Show the agency what you can actually deliver — the cert just tells them which door to open.

4. **Filter SAM.gov by set-aside type every week.** Your cert category unlocks a specific column of opportunities. If you're not checking those filters, the contract goes to someone who is.

5. **Position for teaming before the prime comes looking.** Large primes need certified subs to hit their small business subcontracting goals. Be on their radar proactively, not reactively.

The cert opens the door. You still have to walk through it.
