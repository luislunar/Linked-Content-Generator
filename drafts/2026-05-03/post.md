Nobody talks enough about how long the GovCon pipeline actually takes.

Not weeks. Not months. Often more than a year from first contact to first payment.

Here's the honest timeline most people don't share:

→ **Month 1–2:** SAM.gov active, GSA Schedule application submitted. Nothing happens yet.
→ **Month 3–6:** GSA review process. Back-and-forth on pricing, compliance, documentation.
→ **Month 6–12:** Schedule awarded. Now you need to be found, shortlisted, and quoted.
→ **Month 9–18:** First serious quote opportunities, assuming you're actively marketing to agencies.
→ **Month 12–24:** First award, if you've been consistent about capability statements, relationships, and following up on sources sought.

This isn't a reason not to pursue it. GSA Schedule revenue compounds — year 3 looks nothing like year 1.

But small businesses that go in expecting quick revenue get frustrated and quit at month 8, right before the pipeline starts paying out.

The ones who win treat the first 12 months as relationship-building, not revenue-generating.

How long did it take you to see your first federal revenue — or where are you in the timeline right now?
