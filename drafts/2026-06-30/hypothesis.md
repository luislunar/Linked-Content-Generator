This draft tests the `alert` hook pattern on `compliance` content using a `tips_list` format. After two consecutive cream-background story posts, the dark navy `single-06-checklist` template creates visual feed contrast. The hook opens with a blunt consequence ("Your GSA Schedule doesn't renew itself") before pivoting to a numbered list of annual obligations — a format known to perform on LinkedIn because it's scannable, completeness-signaling, and immediately actionable.

The five items were chosen for specificity (real deadlines, real consequences) rather than vague reminders — the Nov 30 Annual Sales Report in particular is the most missed deadline in GovCon, which should generate recognition-driven comments. The CTA asks for a near-miss experience rather than an opinion, which invites personal storytelling and increases comment depth. Expected to move: **reactions** (structured checklist format is visually satisfying and shareable) and **comments** (near-miss framing invites vulnerability — contractors will admit what caught them off guard).
