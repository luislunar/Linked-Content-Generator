Your GSA Schedule doesn't renew itself.

And yet, every year I watch small businesses lose their Schedule — not because they failed to win contracts, but because they missed a compliance deadline they didn't know existed.

Here are 5 things you must do annually to keep your Schedule alive:

1. **Renew your SAM.gov registration** — it expires every 12 months. If it lapses, your Schedule freezes and federal payments stop. No warning from GSA.

2. **Accept Mass Modifications** — GSA periodically issues mass mods that update contract terms. Accepting them is a legal obligation, not optional. Ignore them and you're technically in violation.

3. **Audit your pricing for price reduction liability** — if you've lowered your commercial rates since your last award, you may owe GSA a price reduction. Ignoring it triggers a compliance review.

4. **Update your GSA Advantage pricelist** — contracting officers search Advantage before they issue an RFQ. A stale pricelist signals an abandoned contract. It costs you opportunities silently.

5. **Submit your Annual Sales Report by November 30** — this is the most missed deadline in GovCon. Miss it and GSA can terminate your Schedule for cause.

Most contractors don't have a calendar entry for any of these. That's how Schedules die quietly.
