A business owner called me a few years back.

She'd been running a professional services firm for six years. $3M in revenue. Solid team, strong client list. She wanted to get into the federal market. But she hadn't started the GSA Schedule process.

When I asked why, she said: "Honestly? I figured we were too small. The government wants the big players. We don't have the clout."

I've heard that exact sentence more times than I can count.

Here's what I told her: the GSA Multiple Award Schedule was built for companies exactly like hers. Agencies have mandatory small business spending goals. Contracting officers are actively looking for qualified vendors in the $1M–$10M range — often because the large primes are over-committed, unavailable, or simply don't have the specialized expertise a particular requirement needs.

She wasn't too small. She was precisely the profile federal buyers are supposed to be supporting.

Eight months later, her Schedule was approved.

Fourteen months after that, her first federal award came in.

Not because she was lucky, or well-connected, or had a massive BD team. Because she stopped letting a story run her decision.

The belief that "we're too small for this" has quietly kept more small businesses out of the federal market than any paperwork, any timeline, or any compliance requirement ever has.

Eligibility is more accessible than most people assume. The gap is usually information — not qualification.
