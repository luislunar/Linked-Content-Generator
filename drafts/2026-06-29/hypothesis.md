This draft tests the `story` hook pattern on the `myths` topic using a `personal_story` format (pure prose, no bullets). The central hypothesis is that a specific first-person narrative — a named professional services firm owner, a concrete revenue figure ($3M), a real quote ("We don't have the clout."), and a clear outcome (Month 14 first award) — will outperform the more common insight-list format on this topic.

The myth addressed ("we're too small for federal work") directly maps to the ICP's top fear: "I don't know if my company qualifies." By showing a peer who shared that fear and succeeded anyway, the post targets both reactions (identification/validation) and comments (the CTA invites readers to share their own myths, which is inherently low-friction and specific enough to generate real responses). Expected lift: +comments vs. baseline, because the CTA asks for a personal confession rather than an opinion, and the story structure models the vulnerability needed to reply.
