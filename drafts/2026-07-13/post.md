When did you last actually audit your SAM.gov profile — not just hit renew?

After 18 years of running these reviews, I can tell you: there's a real difference.

Inside every SAM.gov renewal we handle at GSA Focus, we work through a 47-point checklist. NAICS codes. UEI and CAGE cross-check. Bank routing for payment. Registered address against entity profile. SAM.gov doesn't flag errors — it accepts whatever you submit and moves on. You only find out something's wrong when opportunities stop matching, or when a Contracting Officer can't verify your profile during a source selection.

Last month I was in GSA eOffer/eMod reviewing a client's registration. Their primary NAICS code hadn't been updated in three years. They'd added a new service line but never reflected it in their SAM profile. Invisible to them. Visible to every CO searching for vendors in that category. We caught it before the renewal window closed, filed the update, and got them current.

The registration that looks "done" is often the one quietly working against you.
