7 things about government contracting that almost everyone believes — and that are wrong.

I believed 5 of them when I started. Each one costs someone $50K+.

1. "You need a lobbyist or inside connection."
    → 94% of first-time GSA Schedule awards in FY24 went to firms with zero federal relationships.

2. "Set-asides (WOSB, SDVOSB, 8(a)) are automatic wins."
    → They restrict the pool. You still compete on price + past performance.

3. "You have to be cheap to win."
    → Too cheap = rejected as fast as too expensive. Winning pricing is the 40th–60th percentile.

4. "Federal payment is slow — budget 90+ days."
    → Prompt Payment Act requires 30 days. Median actual: 22 days.

5. "End of FY (September) is when all the money is."
    → Q4 is 28% of obligations. Q2 (Feb–Mar) is the second-biggest quarter. Don't sit idle 9 months.

6. "You need 50+ employees to be credible."
    → Largest category of Schedule holders: 1–9 employees (38% of total).

7. "Consultants can get you on Schedule in 30 days."
    → Fastest legitimate path is ~4 months. Anyone promising 30 is cutting compliance corners. Walk away.

If you've been sitting on the fence because of #1, #3, or #6 — the fence doesn't exist.
