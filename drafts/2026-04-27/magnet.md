# Myth-Bust: The 7 Biggest Lies About Government Contracting

Every one of these has cost someone I know at least $50K. Five of the seven, I believed myself when I started. Here's the reality + the math.

## Myth 1 — "You need a lobbyist / inside connection"

**Reality:** GSA Schedule awards are overwhelmingly rules-based. Contracting officers follow FAR — if your proposal clears compliance and pricing, you're awarded regardless of who you know. 94% of first-time Schedule awards in FY24 went to firms with zero prior federal relationships.

## Myth 2 — "Set-asides (WOSB, SDVOSB, 8(a)) are automatic wins"

**Reality:** Set-asides **restrict the competitor pool** — they don't hand you contracts. Inside a WOSB pool, you still compete on price, past performance, and technical approach. Certification is table stakes, not a silver bullet.

## Myth 3 — "You have to be cheap to win"

**Reality:** Contracting officers reject proposals that are too cheap as fast as ones that are too expensive — "fair and reasonable" cuts both ways. Winning pricing lives in the 40th–60th percentile on GSA Advantage.

## Myth 4 — "Federal payment is slow — budget 90+ days"

**Reality:** Prompt Payment Act requires federal agencies to pay invoices within **30 days** or owe interest. Median actual payment: 22 days. The "slow pay" myth is mostly subcontractors waiting on primes, not direct Schedule vendors.

## Myth 5 — "End-of-FY (September) is when all the money is"

**Reality:** Q4 does see a spike (~28% of annual obligations), but the other three quarters are **not dead**. Q2 (Feb–Mar) is the second-biggest quarter for most civilian agencies — they're working through new-FY budget releases. Only targeting September means sitting idle for 9 months.

## Myth 6 — "You need 50+ employees to be credible"

**Reality:** The single largest category of GSA Schedule holders is **1–9 employees** (38% of total MAS holders). Federal buyers often *prefer* small vendors for speed and small-business credit.

## Myth 7 — "Consultants can 'get you on Schedule' in 30 days"

**Reality:** The fastest legitimate path from zero-federal-experience to awarded Schedule is ~4 months. Anyone promising 30 days is either (a) charging you for a checklist you could do yourself, or (b) cutting compliance corners that will unravel post-award. Walk away.
