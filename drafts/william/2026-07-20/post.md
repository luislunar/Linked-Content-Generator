A question I ask on almost every discovery call:

*"Once you're on the Schedule, how are you planning to find work?"*

Most people pause. A few say they'll check SAM.gov when they have time. A handful mention a contact or two at an agency they've worked with commercially.

What I almost never hear is a real answer.

Here's what I've noticed after talking to hundreds of small businesses going through this process — the ones who actually generate federal revenue don't wait for opportunities to find them. They show up before the solicitation exists.

That looks like:

→ Responding to Sources Sought notices (even when you can't win the prime — you're leaving a footprint)
→ Tracking contracting officers at target agencies, not just watching GSA Advantage
→ Setting up SAM.gov opportunity alerts the day the Schedule is approved
→ Knowing which contracts in your NAICS are recompeting in the next 12–18 months

One company I worked with came in thinking the Schedule was the finish line. Month one, nothing. Month two, nothing. Month three, he started mapping recompetes and responding to every Sources Sought in his space. Month eight: first award.

The Schedule is the access. The pipeline is what actually gets you paid.

What does your opportunity-tracking look like right now — are you building it before the solicitation drops, or mostly reacting after?
