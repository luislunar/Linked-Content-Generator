A business owner I spoke to last fall had been sitting on a GSA Schedule for 14 months.

Zero awards. Not even a request for quote.

She told me on our call: "I've been waiting for the phone to ring." And honestly? I get it. You put months of work into the application, you get approved, and you assume the contracts follow.

They don't work that way.

What shifted for her was moving from passive to active:

→ She started responding to every Sources Sought notice in her NAICS category — not to win immediately, but to put her name in front of agencies before the solicitation dropped.

→ She attended two industry days last Q3. One conversation led to a program manager sending her a direct RFQ three months later.

→ She set up a saved search on SAM.gov for expiring contracts in her space — recompetes are often the fastest path to a first award because the agency already knows the requirement.

By month 17, she had her first federal contract. Not because she got lucky. Because she started treating her Schedule as a starting point, not a finish line.

The business owners I talk to who are actually winning have one thing in common: they show up consistently, even before anyone is paying attention.
