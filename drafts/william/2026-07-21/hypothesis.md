This draft tests whether a short truth-hook hot_take reframing the GSA Schedule as a credential (not a contract) drives more comments than the recent pipeline and client_story posts. The "what do you wish someone had told you before applying?" CTA is designed to invite personal confession-style stories, which tend to generate longer comment threads than outcome-sharing prompts. Expected score movers: reactions should be solid because this misconception is universal among first-time prospects; comments could outperform the pipeline posts because the question invites anyone who has been through the process to share a regret or lesson. The short length (under 180 words) is a deliberate test of whether compact truth posts retain engagement comparable to the 240-word story-format posts used recently.
