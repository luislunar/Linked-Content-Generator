I was on a call a few weeks ago with the owner of a facilities management company out of Georgia.

He'd had a GSA Schedule for almost two years. Hadn't won a federal contract yet — but he'd done the SAM.gov registration right and was waiting for the right opportunity. Then a contracting officer reached out. An agency in the southeast had found his company through a market research search and wanted to send an RFQ. He called me excited.

By the time I looked into it, the excitement was gone. His SAM registration had expired three weeks earlier. No email from SAM.gov, no warning, no grace period for the RFQ to pause. The CO had moved on.

Here's what I tell every prospect after hearing this kind of story: SAM.gov registration expires every 365 days. No automatic renewal. No reliable reminder you can count on. Just a quiet lapse that can block you from receiving federal payments — or even being found by a CO — at exactly the wrong moment. I've heard versions of this on probably a dozen discovery calls.

Check your expiration date today. Renew at least 60 days early — processing takes 2–4 weeks and the federal calendar doesn't wait for anyone. And while you're in there, confirm your NAICS codes still match what your company actually does.

The opportunity was real. The registration wasn't ready. Don't let that be your story.
