Nobody talks about what actually holds most small businesses back from applying to the GSA Schedule.

It's not the paperwork. It's not the cost.

On almost every first call I take, the real question hiding behind "is this worth it?" is: *What if we do all this and still don't win anything?*

That fear is valid. And it deserves a real answer, not a sales pitch.

The GSA Schedule doesn't guarantee contracts — it gets you onto the field. Every federal buyer gains simplified access to your rates, your capabilities, your company. That's the infrastructure.

The small businesses I talk to that win consistently didn't wait to "figure out contracting" after getting approved. They were building relationships, showing up on SAM.gov, responding to Sources Sought — while the application was still in process.

The Schedule opens the door. What you build on it is the work.

What was the real hesitation holding you back before you started the application process — or picked up the phone to ask? I'd genuinely like to know.
