### CTAs que generan comentarios

- `"What's been your experience with [tema]?"` — invita a compartir historia real
- `"Which of these are you already doing? Which one are you adding this week?"` — accionable
- `"Drop the biggest myth you believed about GovCon when you first started."` — nostálgico + comunidad
- `"What's kept you going when [dificultad]?"` — emocional, genera historias
- `"When did you last check your SAM.gov registration? Be honest. 😅"` — humor + acción concreta
- `"What's the one thing holding your business back from showing up? Fill in the blank: ______"` — participativo

### Lo que NO hacen estos posts

- Sin CTAs de descarga ("comenta X para recibir el PDF")
- Sin imágenes (texto puro — el hook hace el trabajo)
- Sin párrafos largos (máx 2–3 oraciones seguidas)
- Sin jerga sin explicar
- Sin superlativo sin datos
- Hashtags: 3–5 máximo, siempre incluir `#govcon` y `#govconcommunity`

### Post 1 — Teaming/Subcontracting (64 reactions, 8 comments)
```
"There's a myth in GovCon that you need to have everything figured out before you can compete. You don't."

Teaming and subcontracting exist because no single company possesses all necessary capabilities.

→ Prime contractors hold the contract and remain accountable to the agency
→ Subcontractors deliver specific work scopes under the prime
→ Teaming agreements enable companies to partner before contract awards

This is one of the fastest ways to build past performance, get inside a contract vehicle,
and learn how federal work actually gets done.

The key is knowing what you bring to the table and being clear about it. Agencies and primes
seek companies who do something really well — not companies trying to do everything.

Know your lane. Get good at it. Then go find partners who need exactly that. That's how you get in the door.

"What's been your experience with teaming as a prime or a sub?"

#govcon #federalcontracting #teaming #sub #prime #govconcommunity #smallbusiness
```

### Post 3 — SAM.gov checklist (49 reactions, 12 comments)
```
"SAM.gov is not optional. I know that sounds blunt, but I can't tell you how many small businesses miss out on federal contracts because their registration lapsed or was never done correctly to begin with."

Here's a quick SAM.gov health check:

→ Registration is active (renews annually—set a reminder NOW)
→ NAICS codes reflect actual capabilities
→ Business size designations are accurate
→ Points of contact are current
→ Representations & certifications are up to date
→ Exclusions check is clean

"An expired or incomplete SAM.gov registration can disqualify you from award even if you submitted the best proposal in the pile. Don't let admin be the reason you lose."

"When did you last check your SAM.gov registration? Be honest. 😅"

### Post 4 — SAM.gov como roadmap (45 reactions, 11 comments)
```
"You're leaving contracts on the table and SAM.gov is trying to tell you where they are."

Most small businesses use SAM.gov ineffectively, relying on keyword searches alone. Here's a better approach:

1. Strategic Filtering: Combine NAICS codes, PSC codes, and set-aside filters to reduce noise
2. Competitive Intelligence: Pull award data in your sector to identify winning competitors, contract values, and relevant agencies — free and public
3. Recompete Pipeline: Track expiring contracts as upcoming opportunities; positioning early beats reactive chasing

"SAM.gov isn't just a database. It's a roadmap if you know how to read it."

### Post 8 — Past performance myth (26 reactions, 17 comments)
```
"You need years of past performance before you can win a federal contract." I hear this one constantly. And it stops people before they even start.

Past performance matters, yes. But it's not the only path in. Here's what most people don't know:

→ If you have no federal past performance, relevant commercial experience can count
→ Subcontracting lets you build past performance while someone else holds the prime
→ Some set-aside contracts and simplified acquisitions have lower past performance thresholds
→ New businesses can sometimes be evaluated on key personnel experience instead

The government wants to bring in capable small businesses. There are programs literally designed to help you get that first contract.

Don't let a myth be the reason you don't pursue the contract. Do the research. Understand the evaluation criteria. Find the right opportunity for where you are right now.

"What's a GovCon myth you've heard that held you back?"

#govcon #govconmyths #smallbusiness #community #experience
```

### Post 9 — AI compliance en GovCon (24 reactions, 10 comments)
```
"The government just changed the rules on AI. Here's what small businesses need to know."

GSA has proposed an AI clause for federal contracts with four key requirements:

1. Disclose every AI tool used in contract performance
2. Flow compliance requirements down to subs and vendors
3. Report security incidents within 72 hours
4. Grant government broad data rights, including custom AI developments

"If you're using AI to build workflows, write proposals, or deliver work — the government may claim rights to what it produces."

The rule isn't final yet, but adoption is coming. Small businesses that prepare now will be positioned competitively.

"Are you paying attention to the AI compliance landscape in your pipeline?"

#govcon #aicompliance #AIGovCon #govconeducation
```
